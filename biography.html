Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer and one of the most successful batsmen in modern cricket. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest players of his generation. Kohli has represented India in all formats of the game including Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is). He is known for his aggressive batting style, remarkable consistency, and strong leadership qualities.

Over the years, Kohli has broken numerous cricket records and has been one of the most dominant players in international cricket. His fitness standards, passion for the sport, and ability to chase targets have made him a role model for aspiring cricketers around the world.

Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. His father Prem Kohli was a criminal lawyer, and his mother Saroj Kohli is a homemaker. Kohli grew up in a middle-class family and developed an interest in cricket at a very young age.

His father noticed his passion for cricket and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was just nine years old. Under the guidance of coach Rajkumar Sharma, Kohli began developing his cricket skills and soon became one of the most talented young players in Delhi.

During his early years, Kohli participated in several local tournaments and school competitions. His performances in these tournaments helped him gain recognition and opened the path for his professional cricket career.

Education

Virat Kohli studied at Vishal Bharti Public School in Delhi. Later, he joined Saviour Convent School to focus more on his cricket training and development.

Although education was important, Kohli's dedication to cricket gradually became stronger. His passion for the game eventually led him to pursue cricket as a professional career.

Domestic Career

Kohli started his domestic cricket journey by playing for the Delhi Under-15 team in 2002. He later played for the Under-17 and Under-19 teams where his performances impressed many cricket selectors.

In 2006, Kohli made his debut for the Delhi senior team in the Ranji Trophy. His batting technique, confidence, and ability to score runs consistently helped him establish himself as a promising young cricketer.

In 2008, Kohli captained the Indian Under-19 team and led them to victory in the ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia. This achievement marked an important milestone in his early career.

International Career

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008 in an ODI match against Sri Lanka. Initially, he was seen as a young talent, but his consistent performances quickly established him as a key member of the Indian team.

Kohli was part of the Indian squad that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup. Over the years, he became one of the most reliable batsmen in world cricket and scored numerous centuries across all formats.

He is especially known for his ability to chase difficult targets and perform under pressure. His batting technique, mental strength, and aggressive style have made him one of the most successful cricketers of modern times.

Captaincy

Virat Kohli became the captain of the Indian Test team in 2014 after the retirement of Mahendra Singh Dhoni from Test cricket. Later, he also became the captain of the ODI and T20 teams.

Under Kohli's leadership, India achieved several important victories, including historic Test series wins overseas. He also emphasized fitness and discipline within the team, which significantly improved the overall performance of Indian cricket.

Personal Life

Virat Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017 in a private ceremony in Italy. The couple is widely admired and often appears together at public events.

In January 2021, they welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika. Kohli is also known for promoting fitness and healthy living.
